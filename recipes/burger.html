Burger

Description

A burger is a popular food consisting of a cooked, round patty (usually made of ground beef, chicken, or plant-based protein)
that is fried or grilled.

Ingredients

  • Meat: 500g (1 lb) ground beef or chuck (80% lean / 20% fat)
  • Binder: 1 large egg
  • Texture: 30g (1/2 cup) panko breadcrumbs or crushed saltine crackers
  • Flavor Boosters: 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • For Serving: 4 brioche buns, cheese slices, lettuce, sliced tomatoes, red onion, and condiments (mayo, ketchup, mustard)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Mixture: In a large bowl, lightly combine the ground beef, egg, breadcrumbs, Worcestershire sauce, salt,
    and pepper. Do not overmix, or the burgers will become dense and tough.
  2. Shape the Patties: Divide the mixture into 4 equal portions. Gently shape them into patties about 1.5 cm larger than your
    buns, as they will shrink while cooking. Press a small thumbprint or indentation into the center of each patty to prevent them from
    puffing up into meatball shapes on the grill.
  3. Cook: Heat a cast-iron skillet, heavy pan, or outdoor grill over medium-high heat. Add a light drizzle of oil if pan-frying. Cook the
    patties for 3 to 4 minutes on the first side without pressing down.
  4. Flip and Add Cheese: Flip the patties, place your cheese slice on top, and cook for another 3 to 4 minutes until the cheese is melted
    and the burger reaches an internal temperature of 71°C (160°F).
  5. Toast the Buns: While the meat rests for 2-3 minutes, place the halved brioche buns cut-side down in the hot skillet for 1-2 minutes
    until golden and toasted.
  6. Assemble: Spread your preferred sauces on the toasted buns, layer with lettuce, tomato, onion, and the cheesy patty.
